    Habitat

    It favours open country where deciduous woods alternate with flat spreading expanses. It nests in the Iberian Peninsula, southeastern Europe and as far as northwestern India and Mongolia.

    Behaviour

    When perched, the Eastern Imperial Eagle seems to be lazy or indolent. It spends most of the day perched, as asleep, on a branch in tree or on high pole.

    Diet

    Eastern Imperial Eagle hunts rabbits and hares, but also fairly large birds, snakes and lizards. It also consumes carrion and recently dead domestic animals.
